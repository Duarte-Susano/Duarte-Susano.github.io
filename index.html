You've reached the end. It's a secret to everybody, so thank you for taking the time to get here! That aside, they told me the initial blurb had to be short, so if for some reason you were left itching to know more, well this is the section for you, i.e. the section where people talk about fundamentally objective things, such as:

  • Top 10 games of all time
  • Best music genre
  • And much, much more

Addressing the elephant in the room first, we're running an honest website over here, so there is no such thing as "favourite game" and/or similar concepts, and if you disagree, try putting SMB3 and The Witness side to side and we'll see who's flabbergasted then. That said, I never refuse the opportunity to shoutout some of the good folks who've done so much stellar work over the years, so without running late, here's the shoutout track straight from my mixtape:

  • Shoutout to Treasure, and whatever's rest of them
  • To the late Team Ico, and to what their future may bring
  • To the OG ragtag bunch that used to make up Game Freak
  • To all the Capcom divisions, sub-teams, and spun-off studios
  • To Looking Glass studios and everything adjacent to them
  • To all the early 2000's Sega subsidiaries and everyone involved

And to everyone else who would rightfully make the list, but would so increasing its size to non-supported browser lengths. Special edge case to everyone making Cave Story clones, or so-called "indies", for keep pushing the medium forward. Sorry for putting you all in one bag, but as Guile says when he wins in SF2 (sort of), "All games are great".

Moving on to non-game related things, you can probably catch me scouring around the internet, most likely on some Wikipedia article, which might or might not correlate to some major interests. Biology is always a big recurrent topic though; I didn't pick the class three years straight just because it was easier than Physics (I swear I like my plants!).

If I'm not wandering around looking for things to read, I'm probing around for things to listen to. Never forget that Bandcamp is your friend (hope this ages well), from progression defying Dream Theater to the minimalism of Steve Reich, which is my best shot at avoiding the above-mentioned statement. Chances are though, if it has an MF DOOM style accordion, I'm probably in. Special shoutout to the UK club scene, giving us straight bangers for countless decades.

Since there isn't anything that lasts forever though, not even well thought out rants, I'll wrap it up here for everyone's sake. If you scrolled all the way down in hopes of finding some last hidden project from the portfolio, you're in luck because it's right here, under your nose. Heads-up though, I'm not a big "browser person". All in all, that's about it. What other secrets may be lying ahead hidden in plain sight? Were there more than two arcs?
